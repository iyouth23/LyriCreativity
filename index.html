About LyriCreativity

LyriCreativity: Origins

I have always been fascinated with extracting data from my favorite music: which artists I play the most, which genres I listen to the most, and the like. I developed LyriCreativity to extend this data-gathering hobby, and to expose others to the fun inherent in data mining.

What Does LyriCreativity Do?

LyriCreativity assesses the creativity of a song by calculating the percentage of lyrics that are unique in that song. You can use LyriCreativity to rate the creativity of your favorite songs by how repetitive the lyrics are.

How Does LyriCreativity Work?

LyriCreativity does the following in order to gauge a song's creativity:

  1. Extracts song lyrics with the assistance of a public API
  2. Standardizes song lyrics for analysis (removing unnecessary punctuation and formatting, and keeping only the words themselves)
  3. Calculates the number of words in the song
  4. Calculates the number of unique words that exist in the song
  5. Computes the number of unique words as a percentage of total words
  6. Classifies a song as "not creative," "somewhat creative," or "very creative" based on this percentage

LyriCreativity classifies a song as follows:

Percent Unique Song Lyrics Creativity Level
(0%, 40%) Not Creative
[40%, 60%) Somewhat Creative
[60%, 100%] Very Creative

Case Study 3: Modern Alternative Rock

Artist: of Montreal
Song: nursing slopes

Biography and Background A lesser known but still commercially successful alternative band, of Montreal is recognized for hits like Gronlandic Edit and Wraith Pinned to the Mist and Other Games. The group has always been known for their eclectic lyrics and novel composition structures. nursing slopes showcases of Montreal's creative side: there are two verses and just one instance of what might be considered the chorus. With seldom used words like "anthers" and "polymorphic" in the song, it is no wonder that LyriCreativity gives high creativity marks to nursing slopes.
